Processing video




Processing a video means, performing operations on the video frame by frame. Frames are nothing but just the particular instance of the video in a single point of time.
We may have multiple frames even in a single second. Frames can be treated as similar to an image.
So, whatever operations we can perform on images can be performed on frames as well.

Computer graphics often use the RBG model, which stands for Red, Green and Blue. These are the three primary colors that can be used to create other colors.
The main thing is different colors formed by combining these primary colors. RGB colors usually have values of 0-255, where 0 means the color isn’t present at all, and 255 means it’s present with full strength.
